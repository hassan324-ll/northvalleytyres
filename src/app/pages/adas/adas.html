ADAS Radar Calibration

Advanced Driver Assistance Systems help your vehicle react faster and drive safer. After windscreen replacement, wheel alignment, or collision repair, accurate ADAS calibration is essential to keep those systems working correctly.

What Is ADAS?

ADAS includes sensors, radar, and cameras designed to monitor traffic, speed, lane position, and distance.

These systems support the driver with warnings and, in some cases, automatic braking or steering assistance.

Different ADAS Systems

ADAS covers a wide range of driver assistance technologies. Names can vary by manufacturer, but core functions are similar.

Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC)

ACC maintains your chosen speed and adjusts automatically with traffic flow by using radar or camera data.

Autonomous Emergency Braking (AEB)

AEB scans ahead for hazards, warns the driver, and can apply strong braking if no response is detected.

Forward Collision Warning (FCW)

FCW identifies potential impacts and alerts the driver early, helping reduce stopping distance and collision risk.

Lane Departure Warning / LKAS

This system alerts drivers when drifting from the lane and, on some vehicles, provides gentle steering correction.

Blind Spot Detection

Blind spot monitoring warns when another vehicle is alongside, and some systems actively help prevent unsafe lane changes.

Road Sign Detection

A forward-facing camera reads road signs and shows updates on the instrument display to support safer, compliant driving.

Park Assist

Park Assist can identify suitable spaces and support steering inputs to make difficult parking manoeuvres easier and safer.
